SimGym — Internal Playbook

Incorporation

Zero-to-Delaware. How to go from nothing → LLP → design partner revenue → US C-Corp, in the right order.

Phase 0

Starting Point

No entity. No contracts. Just two founders and an idea. This is where we begin.

Current State

No LLP, no Delaware C-Corp, no design partner signed, no revenue. Goal of this playbook: get to money in bank and a clean legal structure, without burning cash prematurely.

Phase 1

Register the LLP

Do this first. LLP is the minimum viable legal entity to sign contracts and receive money in India. Cost: ~₹10,000. Time: 1–2 weeks.

01

Choose a name and check availability

Go to MCA portal (mca.gov.in). Search for your LLP name. You need to reserve it via RUN-LLP (Reserve Unique Name). Have 2–3 alternatives ready.

02

Get a CA to file the incorporation

Hire a CA or use an online service (Razorpay Rize, Vakilsearch, IndiaFilings). They handle Form FiLLiP and get you a Certificate of Incorporation.

  • Cost: ₹5,000–₹12,000 all-in
  • Documents needed: PAN, Aadhaar, address proof for both founders
  • You'll also need a registered office address (can be home)
03

Open LLP current account

Once COI (Certificate of Incorporation) is in hand, open a current account at any bank (HDFC, ICICI, or Kotak recommended for startup friendliness). This is where design partner money lands.

04

Get LLP PAN and GST (if needed)

PAN for the LLP: mandatory for invoicing. GST: register only if revenue crosses ₹20L or if clients require it. Most early design partners won't require GST; skip until needed.

Phase 2

Close the Design Partner (via LLP)

Before any US entity. Prove the product has paying demand first. This is validation, not just revenue.

01

Pick your offer structure

Two clean options — pick one and stick to it:

  • Option A (recommended): Paid Design Partner — ₹2L–₹10L for 2–3 months. One-time project fee. Simple.
  • Option B: Discounted SaaS — ₹50k–₹2L/month. More recurring, harder to close early.

Do not offer equity. Do not complicate it.

02

Sign a Design Partner Agreement

Not a heavy MSA. A simple 1–2 page agreement is enough at this stage.

DESIGN PARTNER AGREEMENT
This Agreement is entered into on [Date] between:

[Your LLP Name], an LLP registered in India ("Company")
AND
[Client Name], [a company / an individual] ("Client")
1. Scope
The Company will provide early access to its [product/service], including
customisations and support, to help the Client evaluate and shape the product.
2. Term
This agreement is valid for [3 months], starting from [Start Date].
3. Fees
Client agrees to pay:
  - Total Fee: ₹[Amount]
  - Payment Terms: [100% upfront] / [50% upfront + 50% after 30 days]
4. Deliverables
Company will:
  - Provide access to the product
  - Deliver agreed custom workflows / features
  - Conduct weekly feedback calls
5. Design Partnership Nature
Client agrees to:
  - Provide feedback and usage insights
  - Act as a reference customer (optional, mutually agreed)
6. IP Ownership
All intellectual property developed remains exclusively with the Company.
7. Confidentiality
Both parties agree to keep shared information confidential during and
after the term of this agreement.
8. Liability
Company provides the product "as-is" during this pilot phase.
Liability is limited to fees paid under this agreement.
9. Termination
Either party may terminate with 15 days' written notice.
10. Governing Law
This agreement is governed by the laws of India.
Signatures
For [LLP Name]:                     For [Client Name]:

_______________________             _______________________
[Founder Name]                      [Client Signatory]
[Title], [LLP Name]                 [Title], [Client Name]
Date: _______________               Date: _______________
03

Send invoice and collect payment

Create invoice under LLP name. Include: LLP name, address, PAN, bank details. Tools: Razorpay, Zoho Invoice, or even a clean PDF. Collect into LLP current account. Record as revenue.

Common mistakes at this stage
  • Setting up Delaware C-Corp before closing a deal — burns ₹43k before you have validation. Don't.
  • Offering equity to design partners — messy, unnecessary, and complicates the cap table before you even start.
  • Over-engineering the contract — a 12-page MSA kills momentum. Keep it to 1–2 pages early.
Phase 3

Decide When to Create the Delaware C-Corp

This is a trigger-based decision, not a time-based one. Stay LLP until at least one of these fires.

Pull the trigger only when
  • Applying to YC or a US accelerator
  • Actively talking to a US investor (term sheet incoming)
  • A US customer explicitly requires a US contracting entity
  • Revenue has crossed ₹10–15L and you're scaling fast

If none of these apply yet: stay LLP. The structure cost (₹1L+) is real. The compliance overhead is real. Don't pre-optimize.

Phase 4

Set Up Delaware C-Corp (Stripe Atlas)

When the trigger fires, this is the fastest path. Total cost: ~$500 (~₹43,000). Time: 1 week.

01

Go to Stripe Atlas and fill the form

  • Company name (check Delaware name availability first)
  • Founder details (name, address, passport or Aadhaar)
  • Equity split — keep it simple: 50/50 or whatever you've agreed
02

Pay $500 and wait

You receive: Delaware C-Corp incorporation documents, EIN (may take 2–4 weeks), and access to US banking setup.

03

Open US bank account via Mercury or Brex

Atlas routes you directly. Mercury is free and fully remote-friendly for Indian founders. No US address or SSN required. Takes 1–3 days.

Phase 5

Clean Up the Structure

After Delaware is set up you'll have two entities running in parallel. That's fine temporarily, but clean it up within 3–6 months.

Target structure

You → Delaware C-Corp (US parent) → Indian OpCo (LLP or Pvt Ltd subsidiary)

Right now you have: You → LLP (India) and You → Delaware (US), disconnected. That's the mess to fix.

A

Simple path (early stage)

All new contracts get signed under Delaware C-Corp. LLP slowly becomes inactive. No formal IP transfer needed yet. Works fine until you raise.

B

Clean path (pre-fundraise)

Assign IP formally from LLP → C-Corp. Convert or wind down LLP. LLP becomes a subsidiary or is dissolved. Needs a CA + lawyer. Budget: ₹50k–₹1.5L.

TL;DR — The Exact Sequence

  1. Register LLP — ~₹10k, 1–2 weeks, needed to sign contracts and receive money.
  2. Close a design partner — simple agreement, ₹2L–₹10L, money into LLP account.
  3. Validate, don't incorporate yet — stay LLP until a real US trigger fires.
  4. Set up Delaware C-Corp via Stripe Atlas — $500, 1 week, only when needed.
  5. Open Mercury/Brex US bank account — free, 1–3 days.
  6. Move new contracts to Delaware — simple path first; formal cleanup before fundraise.
